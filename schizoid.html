Solitude by Choice: Schizoid Traits

By Galileo:) 5 min read

Schizoid personality traits define a pervasive pattern of detachment from social relationships and a restricted range of expression of emotions in interpersonal settings. These are the "true loners" of the world.

The Independent Observer

People with Schizoid traits are often misunderstood as shy or depressed, but this is usually not the case. Unlike the Avoidant personality (who wants friends but fears rejection) or the Depressed person (who withdraws due to sadness), the Schizoid individual simply has no desire for closeness. They are content in their own world.

Diagnostic Criteria

  • No Desire for Closeness: Neither desires nor enjoys close relationships, including being part of a family.
  • Solitary Activities: Almost always chooses solitary activities.
  • Low Sexual Interest: Has little, if any, interest in having sexual experiences with another person.
  • Anhedonia: Takes pleasure in few, if any, activities.
  • Lack of Friends: Lacks close friends or confidants other than first-degree relatives.
  • Indifference: Appears indifferent to the praise or criticism of others.
  • Emotional Coldness: Shows emotional coldness, detachment, or flattened affectivity.

Inner World vs. Outer World

While they may seem dull or empty on the outside, many Schizoid individuals have rich, complex internal fantasy lives. They may prefer the characters in their books or games to real people because they are predictable and non-intrusive.
